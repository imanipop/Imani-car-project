Engine

A **car engine** is the heart of a vehicle that converts fuel into mechanical energy to move the car. Most cars use **internal combustion engines (ICE)**, which burn gasoline or diesel to power pistons connected to the drivetrain. Engines vary in type (inline, V-shaped, flat), cylinders, and displacement, and modern cars may also use **electric motors** or **hybrid systems** combining fuel engines with electric power for better efficiency and performance.

Tyre

A **tyre** is the rubber component that fits around a vehicle’s wheel, providing **traction, cushioning, and stability** on the road. Tyres are designed to grip surfaces, absorb shocks from bumps, and support the car’s weight. They come in various types—like **all-season, summer, winter, or performance tyres**—and their tread patterns, size, and material affect handling, fuel efficiency, and safety.

Rim

A **rim** is the metal part of a wheel that holds the tyre in place. It provides **structural support** for the tyre, allowing it to maintain shape under pressure, and connects the tyre to the vehicle’s hub. Rims come in various **sizes, designs, and materials** (like steel or alloy), and they affect both the car’s **appearance** and **performance**, including handling and fuel efficiency.
